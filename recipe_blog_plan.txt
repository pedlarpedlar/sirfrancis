plan for recipes blog:
Great way to get candybird recognition from recipes/community groups that don't allow advertising, and people will come to the website for the recipes.

Recipes page should have a products part (like index page with quick view).

The comments should have pagination.

The recipe should be printable (when sending to print/pdf, only the recipe should show, with some candybird footers).

This will also be a great way to offer the people "Free recipe included in gift!" where a recipe can just be printed out on an A6 and sent with the parcel. Don't forget to send aunty Shamsoe's recipe!

Can users add recipes? Sure - but they must be logged in. Importantly, users who submit recipes must follow the rules according to our policy and take into consideration any copyrighted material (just for legal purposes, say that if anyone thinks this material is copyright to contact recipes@candybird.co.za immediately.)

Users can also send in their recipes to recipes@candybird.co.za to get it posted to our blog, or they can add it themselves online. Recipes are reviewed once a week and may take up to 14 days to get approved/added to our website.

Users can add recipes for food, sweets, and beauty products, and are welcome to include links to their own or another's website to advise where they purchase the items in their recipes.

Privacy: Users may have an option to show their full name and email, or simply their username, on the website. These can be changed in their profile settings.

Recipe categories should be a thing. Take some ideas from allrecipes.com for ideas. make it even simpler.


Basically, the above is a full blown website. Just to get started however, include a few recipes via the database and the index page can link to those for now.
